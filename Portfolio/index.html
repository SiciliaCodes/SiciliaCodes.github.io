About Me

I’m a medical scientist-turned-system integration guru, and a mom to a pint-sized tornado. I hate boredom and thrive on new adventures. My Tarantino obsession is so intense that I secretly fantasize about being a Deadly Viper Assassination Squad member. When I’m not critiquing movies or re-reading scripts with my husband, I’m reflecting on my bizarre career journey—from almost becoming a physician to battling infectious diseases, tutoring biomedical students, diving into biotech, and now integrating systems. I embrace challenges like a pro and believe in doing things the weird way because that’s where the best lessons lie!

Projects

Clostridium Difficile in Children with Haematological Malignancies
This ongoing research, conducted part-time as part of my PhD at UWA, aims to investigate the sources of Clostridium difficile—a significant One Health pathogen—and its severe impact on a particularly vulnerable group of children. The project seeks to understand the pathogen's sources and how it affects these young patients, ultimately contributing to better management and prevention strategies.
